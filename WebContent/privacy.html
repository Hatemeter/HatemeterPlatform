3. Rights of the person concerned

Pursuant to Section III of the GDPR, the person concerned shall be entitled to exercise their right to:

  1. access personal data (the person will therefore has the right to have free information about the personal data held by the Data Controller/Joint Controllers, as well as to obtain a copy thereof in an accessible format);
  2. amend data (we shall take care, upon your request, of amending incorrect or inaccurate data, where the data do not express evaluation elements);
  3. withdraw consent (if you have consented to the processing of your data, you may withdraw your consent at any time and after you have revoked your consent, your data shall no longer be processed);
  4. cancel personal data – right to be forgotten (for example, in case of withdrawal of consent, if there is no other legal basis for data processing);
  5. restrict data processing (in certain cases - dispute the accuracy of the data, within the timeframe necessary for verification; dispute the lawfulness of the processing with refusal to the cancellation; necessity of the party concerned to use their defense rights, while they are no longer useful for the purposes of the processing; in the event that the processing has been denied, while the necessary checks are being carried out - the data will be stored in such a manner that they may be restored if need be, but, in the meantime, cannot be consulted by the Data Controller/Joint Controllers if not in relation to the validity of his or her request for restriction);
  6. deny consent to the processing due to legitimate reasons (under certain circumstances, he/she may in any case object to the processing of data, and in any case it may refuse processing for direct marketing purposes);
  7. data portability (upon request of the person concerned, the data shall be transmitted to the indicated subject in such a format that they can be easily consulted and used);
  8. advance a dispute to the Supervisory Authority (Privacy Authority).
